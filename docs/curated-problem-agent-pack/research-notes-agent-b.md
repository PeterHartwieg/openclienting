# Research Notes - Agent B

## B-001 Budgy Smuggler

- Sector fit: retail, ecommerce
- Evidence basis: monday.com shows 30+ employees; official website is public.
- Problem signal: spreadsheets stopped working once orders scaled into the hundreds and thousands; the team needed better lead, order, and customer-update workflows.
- Sources:
  - https://monday.com/customers/budgy-smuggler
  - https://budgysmuggler.com/pages/about-us
- Risk note: very strong candidate.

## B-002 Madhu's

- Sector fit: hospitality, food service, events
- Evidence basis: Zoho shows 180 full-time employees; public company website is available.
- Problem signal: spreadsheet-based employee management and wider operational-process digitization gaps across complex events and catering workflows.
- Sources:
  - https://www.zoho.com/one/customers/madhu.html
  - https://www.madhus.co.uk/
- Risk note: one of the strongest hospitality candidates in the pool.

## B-003 My Wall Panels

- Sector fit: retail
- Evidence basis: Zoho Commerce lists size 50-60 and the company website is public.
- Problem signal: Magento still left GST accounting and order tracking manual, and customer status queries created heavy support overhead.
- Sources:
  - https://www.zoho.com/commerce/mywallpanels-casestudy.html
  - https://mywallpanels.co.uk/contact/
- Risk note: now strong enough for the advance group.

## B-004 Counter Culture

- Sector fit: food and beverage retail
- Evidence basis: Zoho says the business has 120 employees and the official website is public.
- Problem signal: Salesforce was too heavy and the company had a patchwork of software, email, and spreadsheets that reduced visibility and accountability.
- Sources:
  - https://www.zoho.com/one/customers/counter-culture.html
  - https://counterculturecoffee.com/
- Risk note: strong advance candidate.

## B-005 Rade Street

- Sector fit: apparel retail
- Evidence basis: Zoho POS says the flagship location is supported by more than 25 employees; official Chennai storefront website is public and confirms the retail operation.
- Problem signal: manual pen-and-paper billing for 500+ customers per day led to manipulation risk, hidden losses, and slower operational flow.
- Sources:
  - https://www.zoho.com/en-in/pos/customers/rade-street.html
  - https://radestreet.in/
- Phase 1 decision: advance.

## B-006 Green Endeavour

- Sector fit: food distribution and retail operations
- Evidence basis: Zoho reports 50-200 employees and the official site confirms the company operates food-service and retail supply businesses.
- Problem signal: customer onboarding, retail-outlet auditing, and related processes were too manual and error-prone.
- Sources:
  - https://www.zoho.com/crm/customers/green-endeavour.html
  - https://greenendeavour.com.au/
- Risk note: strong fit for the advance group.

## B-007 Caple

- Sector fit: retail
- Evidence basis: current evidence is weaker; case indicates multiple demo centers and a retail outlet but does not surface headcount in the snippet used.
- Problem signal: pricing inconsistency and fragmented daily operations across locations.
- Source: https://www.zoho.com/one/customers/caple.html
- Risk note: reserve candidate only until stronger SME evidence is added.
